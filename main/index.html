What am I working on?

I am currently a Junior Research Specialist at UC Santa Cruz working with Brenna Mockler,
Prof. K. Auchettl and Prof. E. Ramirez-Ruiz on a project focused on constraining the stellar
diet of SMBH using Tidal Disruption Events. Since the light curves obtained from observations
of these events follow the mass fallback rate, information about the star, black hole (Mockler 2019) ,
and orbital parameters can be derived.
To do this, baysian statistics and Markov-Chain-Monte-Carlo calculations are employed in conjuction
with computer models and streamlined using the Modular Open Source Fitter for Transients (MOSFiT)
My work focuses on combining these statistical outputs with information about the host galaxy and spectroscopic
data to significantly reduce the range of potential combinations of star and black hole properties. Further
enabling better characterizations of the massive black holes and the dense stellar clusters that surround them.
